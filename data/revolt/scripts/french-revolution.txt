A: The French Revolution. A bankrupt monarchy, a hungry city, and the invention of modern politics.
B: In the spring of 1789 the French crown could not pay its debts.
A: It did not come from nowhere. State debt consumed roughly half of annual revenue; the 1788 harvest failed and bread reached 88 percent of a laborer's wage.
B: An absolute monarchy with no representative check; the Estates-General had not met since 1614.
A: Then the spark. A crowd storms the fortress-prison. Royal troops will not fire. The king learns he no longer controls his capital.
B: The Tuileries is stormed, the king suspended, the First Republic proclaimed. Louis XVI is tried and executed in January 1793.
A: The security forces partly broke, and never fully held.
B: It ended in a new autocracy. The revolution turned on its own founders. Roughly 17000 formally executed in the Terror; total deaths including the Vendee and revolutionary wars run far higher.
A: The French Revolution set the template every later revolution would argue with.
B: That is the anatomy. Where it broke from the pattern is the whole story.
