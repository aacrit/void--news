PHASE B1

Ratify the strategy

▼

Tick each decision once you've thought it through. These become locked CEO decisions — every later step assumes them.

decision

void is the master brand. void --news, void --history, void --vision are products under it — never standalone identities. Splitting into separate brands ("Void Vision Inc.") is off the table.

Why: the CLI metaphor already encodes this. Cross-brand brand-impression compounding outperforms three separate identities at any followers-per-account level under 100k.

decision

Multi-pillar feed: news receipts, history vignettes, methodology carousels, void --vision Reels. The 4 / 2 / 2 / 2 rotation already in ig_generator.py accommodates all of them.

Why: ~80% audience overlap across pillars. Three accounts would leak ~70% of cross-pillar reach.

decision

History is a route within the void --news site, not a separate product. /history keeps its burnt-umber palette for visual differentiation.

Why: identical editorial DNA at different timescales. Splitting domains = splitting compound interest on the master brand.

decision

Primary surface: @void.vision YouTube channel. Each video drop becomes a 30-sec Reel on @void.news. No separate IG account.

Why: video is YouTube-native; IG users prefer short-form. Cross-promotion grows both audiences faster than parallel accounts.

decision

Current: void-news.pages.dev hosting /, /history, future /vision. Migration option: void.media as a parent if available.

Why: two domains + two CI pipelines is a real ops cost (per CLAUDE.md). Bad trade for marginal SEO benefit.

PHASE B8

When to break out — trigger conditions

▼

Reserve handles now. Only activate independent identities when one of these triggers fires. Acknowledge each so you don't forget the criteria.

decision

Signal: <20% engagement overlap between content pillars after 60 days. Followers of pillar A do not interact with pillar B at meaningful rate.

Response: consider giving the higher-performing pillar a dedicated IG.

decision

Signal: a second full-time editorial contributor joins, dedicated to one product line.

Response: give them their own account/channel. One person → one account; two people → at most two accounts.

decision

Signal: a product develops a fundamentally different editorial promise. (Hasn't happened to any void --X yet.)

Response: spin it out into its own brand. The void wordmark stays as an endorsement — "from void."

decision

Signal: a product spins into a paid offering (e.g., void --sources as a bias-data API).

Response: dedicated domain, dedicated handle, dedicated docs site. Master brand stays in the header.
